How Machine Learning Helps the Bank

Customer Segmentation

Prioritize high-value groups

The model helps cluster customers into actionable segments so the bank can tailor messaging, offers, and channels around likely responders.

Predictive Lead Scoring

Rank subscription probability

Each customer can be scored by propensity to subscribe, allowing teams to focus human effort on the strongest leads first.

Marketing Optimization

Reduce wasted outreach

Predictive targeting lowers broad campaign waste by concentrating marketing spend on leads most likely to convert.

Revenue Forecasting

Support business planning

By estimating likely conversions, leadership can forecast campaign yield and allocate budget with more confidence.

Continuous Variable Distribution

Age, call duration, and macroeconomic indicators show distinct patterns that help the bank understand which variables are most useful for lead prioritization.

Outlier Analysis (Boxplots)

Outliers in age and call duration highlight the need for preprocessing so the model stays stable and the resulting lead scores remain reliable.

Feature Separability (Pairplot)

Cross-variable patterns reveal where customer classes begin to separate, helping the bank build a more accurate targeting strategy.

Correlation Heatmap

Strong correlations among macroeconomic indicators signal where feature selection matters most to protect the model from overfitting and noise.

Multicollinearity Detection

The Euribor rate and employment variation are highly correlated, so removing redundant inputs improves model clarity and keeps the scorecard easier to trust.

Decision Tree Architecture

The final model partitions customers using demographic, campaign, and macroeconomic signals to support more accurate conversion predictions and lead ranking.
